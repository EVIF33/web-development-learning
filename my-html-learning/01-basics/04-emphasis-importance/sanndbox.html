Elements of emphasis and importance

<em> — (emphasis)

I'm very glad you're not late.

I am very glad that you are not late.

Use when: to change the meaning of a sentence

Do not use: just for cursive text

<strong> — (strong importance)

This liquid is highly toxic.

I'm counting on you. Don't be late!

Use when: critical information

Do not use: just for bold text

Nesting <strong> and <em>

This liquid is highly toxic — if you drink it, you can die.

When to use <i> и <b> instead of <em>/<strong>

<i> (visual italics):

The hummingbird Archilochus colubris is the most common bird. There were exotic words on the menu: soupe à l'oignon.

<b> (visual highlighting):

Use the elements according to their semantic meaning, not their appearance. Product Name: Percolator 2000.

<u> (underline):

Someday I will learn how to write correctly. (It is usually better not to emphasize the association with links)

Outdated tags (DO NOT use):

<big>, <font>, <center>, <strike>

Use CSS instead of them.
